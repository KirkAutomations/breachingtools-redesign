Technology

Engineered to Dominate

Every Double-Tap tool is the product of combat-tested engineering and aerospace-grade materials.

Anti-Blowback Floating Mass

Internal floating mass technology absorbs impact energy and eliminates blowback and vibration. Unique to Double-Tap. Zero energy wasted on the operator.

Aerospace-Grade Materials

Ti-6Al-4V titanium, 6061T6 aluminum, 303 stainless steel, and 4140 chromoly steel. Machined from solid billets—never cast. Stronger at the molecular level.

Hardcoat Anodized Finish

Deep hard anodized aluminum handles at .002" thickness. Second hardest natural substance after diamond. Corrosion-proof. Battle-ready in any environment.

Billet Machined Precision

Every component is CNC-machined from solid metal billets. No casting, no weak points, no air pockets. Uniform grain structure throughout for maximum impact resistance.

Lightest on the Market

Collapsible design with positive locking shaft. Easy grasp handles with lanyard connections. Maximum breach force at minimum operator weight. Every ounce earns its place.

Made in the USA

Every tool manufactured in Fayetteville, North Carolina. American materials. American labor. 98% aluminum recycling rate. Supporting the Americans who serve.

Our Story

Born in Combat. Built in Fayetteville.

BCM Inc. started as a welding company in 2004 in Fayetteville, North Carolina—home of Fort Liberty and the 82nd Airborne. By mid-2006, a master breacher with real-world operational experience shifted the company’s focus to what he knew best: breaching tools that actually work when lives depend on them.

Since January 2007, Double-Tap tools have been in the hands of operators across law enforcement, military, fire departments, and SWAT teams worldwide. The philosophy is simple: build the tool you’d trust your own life to, then make it available to every operator who needs it.

Every tool features the patented anti-blowback floating mass system—a technology that exists because a breacher understood what engineers couldn’t: the difference between hitting a door in a lab and hitting one at 3 AM with your team stacked behind you.
